## 1. Territory Information & Regulatory Authority

### Commonwealth Territory Status

[FACT - GEOGRAPHIC] The Northern Mariana Islands (CNMI) is a **commonwealth in political union** with the United States, located in the Western Pacific Ocean (Micronesia region).

**Commonwealth Characteristics:**
- **Status:** US Commonwealth Territory (distinct from organized/unincorporated territories)
- **Population:** ~50,000 (2025 estimate)
- **Location:** Western Pacific, ~3,800 miles west of Hawaii, north of Guam
- **Main Islands:** Saipan (capital), Tinian, Rota
- **Governance:** Self-governing commonwealth with US federal relationship
- **Federal Jurisdiction:** Subject to applicable federal laws, more autonomy than territories
- **Citizenship:** US citizens (1986 Covenant)

**Commonwealth vs. Territory Distinction:**
[INFERENCE - MEDIUM CONFIDENCE] Commonwealth status (like Puerto Rico) grants:
- Greater self-governance than organized territories (like Guam, USVI)
- Own constitution and local laws
- More control over internal affairs including professional licensing
- Federal government handles defense, foreign affairs
- Subject to applicable federal law but more autonomy in local matters

### Official Board Information

[FACT - BOARD] The Commonwealth Board of Medical Examiners regulates physicians in CNMI. Website: https://www.gov.mp/. (Per state-research-tracker.csv 2026-01-02)

[INFERENCE - HIGH CONFIDENCE] The CNMI medical board operates under:
1. **Commonwealth law** - CNMI Public Law and Commonwealth Code

## 2. CME Requirements - 50 Hours Per 2 Years, ALL Category 1

### Core Requirement

[FACT - FSMB] Northern Mariana Islands requires **50 hours of CME per 2 years, all Category 1**. (FSMB State CME Requirements, documented in state-research-tracker.csv 2026-01-02)

**Breakdown:**
- **Total:** 50 hours per 2-year cycle
- **Category 1 requirement:** 100% (all 50 hours must be Category 1)
- **Category 2:** NOT accepted (strict quality standard)
- **Renewal frequency:** Biennial (every 2 years)

### Strict Category 1 Requirement

[INFERENCE - HIGH CONFIDENCE] CNMI's **100% Category 1 requirement** is notably strict:

**Category 1 Characteristics:**
- Formal CME with testing, assessment, or verification
- AMA PRA Category 1 or AOA Category 1-A credit
- Accredited providers (ACCME, AOA, AAFP, etc.)
- Documented learning outcomes

**NOT Accepted (Category 2):**
- Self-study without assessment
- Journal reading without CME credit
- Informal learning activities
- Non-accredited courses

[INFERENCE - HIGH CONFIDENCE] CNMI's strict 100% Category 1 requirement (like Hawaii and California) ensures high-quality CME but reduces flexibility for remote island physicians.

[INFERENCE - HIGH CONFIDENCE] CNMI faces **severe CME access challenges**, even more than Guam:

**Island Commonwealth Barriers:**
1. **Extreme isolation** - 120 miles north of Guam, ~1,600 miles from major city (Manila or Tokyo)
2. **Very small population** - 50,000 total population cannot support local CME industry
3. **Limited physician community** - Estimated 50-150 physicians (very small)
4. **Travel burden** - Must fly to Guam (1 hour), Hawaii (7+ hours), or mainland US (10+ hours)
5. **Time zone** - GMT+10, same challenge as Guam for live mainland webinars
6. **Economic constraints** - Limited healthcare funding, high travel costs

**Healthcare Infrastructure:**
- Commonwealth Health Center (Saipan) - main hospital
- Small community clinics on Tinian, Rota
- Limited specialty services (many referrals to Guam or Philippines)

## 5. Federal Requirements & Commonwealth Autonomy

### Federal Requirements Apply

[FACT - FEDERAL] As a US commonwealth, CNMI physicians must comply with applicable **federal medical regulations**:

**DEA MATE Act (Effective June 27, 2023):**
- **8 hours** training on opioid/substance use disorders (one-time)
- Applies to all DEA registrants
- **Separate from CNMI's 50-hour requirement**
- Must be completed at DEA renewal

[INFERENCE - HIGH CONFIDENCE] Federal requirements apply to commonwealth territories. CNMI physicians with DEA registration must complete MATE Act in addition to CNMI CME.

**Total CME Burden for DEA-Registered CNMI Physicians:**
