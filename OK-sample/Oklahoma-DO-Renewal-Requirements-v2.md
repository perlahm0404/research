            ## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

            Oklahoma's osteopathic physician licensing renewal system is governed by the Oklahoma Osteopathic Act (62 O.S. § 620-642) and separate from the MD board. Key findings include:

            - **Current Licensed DOs:** 4,401 total (as of March 2025)
            - - **Active Full Licenses:** 4,211 DOs
              - - **CME Requirement:** Minimum 1 hour of Approved Proper Prescribing Courses annually
                - - **Online Renewal:** Licensee portal at https://www.ok.gov/osboe/
                  - - **Primary Authority:** Oklahoma State Board of Osteopathic Examiners (OSBOE)
                    - - **CME Tracking:** CE Broker integration
                     
                      - **Critical Note:** DO and MD boards are SEPARATE entities in Oklahoma. DO physicians cannot renew through the MD board portal.

                                        - ### Statutory Definition of Osteopathic Medicine
                                     
                                        - Per 62 O.S. § 621, osteopathic medicine means:
                                     
                                        - "A system of health care founded by Andrew Taylor Still and based on the theory that the body is capable of making its own remedies against disease and other toxic conditions when it is in normal structural relationship and has favorable environmental conditions and adequate nutrition. Osteopathic medicine utilizes generally accepted physical, pharmacological and surgical methods of diagnosis and therapy while placing strong emphasis on the importance of body mechanics and manipulative methods to detect and correct faulty structure and function."
