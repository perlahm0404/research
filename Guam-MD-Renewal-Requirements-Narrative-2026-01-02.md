# Guam MD License Renewal Requirements - Comprehensive Research

## Executive Summary

The Guam Board of Medical Examiners regulates physicians in Guam (US Territory). Key requirements:

- **Territory Status:** US Territory (not state) - subject to federal oversight
- **CME Requirement:** 100 hours per 2 years
- **Category Requirement:** 25% minimum Category 1 (25 hours Category 1, 75 hours Category 1 or 2)
- **Renewal Cycle:** Biennial (2 years)
- **Regulatory Authority:** Territorial board under Guam government, federal influence
- **Unique Context:** Island territory with limited local CME resources

**Territory Characteristics:**
- **Status:** US Territory (not a state)

## 2. CME Requirements - 100 Hours Per 2 Years

### Core Requirement

[FACT - FSMB] Guam requires **100 hours of CME per 2 years** for physician license renewal. (FSMB State CME Requirements, documented in state-research-tracker.csv 2026-01-02)

[FACT - FSMB] **25% minimum Category 1** - At least 25 hours must be AMA Category 1 or equivalent; remaining 75 hours can be Category 1 or Category 2.

**Breakdown:**
- **Total:** 100 hours per 2-year cycle
- **Category 1 minimum:** 25 hours (25%)
- **Category 1 or 2 flexible:** 75 hours (75%)
- **Renewal frequency:** Biennial (every 2 years)

### Category Requirements Detail

| CME Type | Hours Required | Percentage | Format |
|----------|---------------|------------|--------|
| **Category 1 (minimum)** | 25 hrs | 25% | Formal CME with testing/verification |
| **Category 1 or 2 (flexible)** | 75 hrs | 75% | Formal or informal CME |
| **Total** | 100 hrs | 100% | Biennial requirement |

[INFERENCE - HIGH CONFIDENCE] Guam's 25% Category 1 minimum (rather than 100% Category 1) provides flexibility, recognizing island territory CME access challenges.

## 3. Territory Context: Island CME Access Challenges

### Geographic Isolation

[INFERENCE - HIGH CONFIDENCE] Guam faces unique CME access challenges:

**Island Territory Barriers:**
1. **Geographic isolation** - 3,800 miles from mainland US, 1,500 miles from nearest major city (Manila)
2. **Limited local CME providers** - Small market (170,000 population) cannot support many local CME vendors
3. **Travel burden** - Must fly to Hawaii, mainland US, or Asia for live conferences (6+ hour flights)
4. **Time zone challenges** - Guam is GMT+10, making live mainland US webinars difficult (middle of night)
5. **Cost burden** - International airfare, lodging for mainland conferences expensive

**Healthcare Workforce Context:**
- Small physician community (~300-400 physicians estimated)
- Military medicine presence (US Naval Hospital, Andersen AFB)
- Mix of civilian and military practitioners
- Recruitment/retention challenges for island practice
