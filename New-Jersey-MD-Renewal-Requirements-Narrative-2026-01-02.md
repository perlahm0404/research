## Appendix: Category I vs. Category II CME Explained

### AMA PRA Category System (Historical Context)

[FACT - CME SYSTEM] The American Medical Association Physician's Recognition Award (AMA PRA) credit system historically used Category 1 and Category 2 credits:

**Category 1 Credit (AMA PRA Category 1):**
- Formal, accredited CME from ACCME-accredited providers
- Live activities (lectures, workshops, conferences)
- Enduring materials (online courses, journal articles with CME credit)
- Performance improvement CME
- Requires assessment/evaluation component
- Credit is certified by ACCME-accredited organization

**Category 2 Credit (AMA PRA Category 2 - DISCONTINUED 2018):**
- Self-directed learning activities
- Non-accredited educational activities
- Reading medical journals without formal CME credit
- Teaching activities
- Quality improvement projects
- Physicians self-reported and self-certified hours

[FACT - CME SYSTEM] In 2015, the AMA announced the discontinuation of Category 2 credit, effective 2018. The AMA transitioned to a system focusing on Category 1 credit only, with subcategories for different types of accredited CME.

[CRITICAL ISSUE] **New Jersey's use of "Category II" terminology is problematic because:**
1. AMA discontinued Category 2 credit in 2018
2. New Jersey regulations may use legacy terminology
3. OR New Jersey may have state-specific Category I/II definitions

**VERIFICATION REQUIRED:** Confirm whether New Jersey:
- Uses AMA PRA Category 1 for "Category I"
- Defines "Category II" as state-specific informal/self-directed CME (not AMA Category 2)
- Has updated regulations to reflect post-2018 AMA CME terminology
- Uses alternative credit systems (e.g., AOA Category 1A/1B for osteopathic CME)

[FACT - CME SYSTEM] The American Osteopathic Association (AOA) uses a different category system:

**AOA Category 1A:**
