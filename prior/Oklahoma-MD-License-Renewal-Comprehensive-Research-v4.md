## Executive Summary

Oklahoma regulates medical physicians (MDs) through a unified state board: the **State Board of Medical Licensure and Supervision**. All allopathic physicians are subject to the same renewal requirements regardless of specialty.

### Key Requirements at a Glance

| Requirement | Detail |
|---|---|
| **CME Total** | 60 hours |
| **CME Cycle** | Every 3 years (triennial) |
| **Category** | Category 1 CME (AMA/AOA credit) |
| **Pain/Opioid Requirement** | 1 hour minimum annually |
| **Renewal Frequency** | At regular intervals, no less than annually |
| **Renewal Cycle** | Triennial (3 years) |
| **Board** | State Board of Medical Licensure and Supervision |
| **Portal** | OBMLS Online License Renewal (pay.apps.ok.gov/medlic/md) |

            ## Section B: Renewal Cycle & Frequency

            ### Renewal Frequency and Timing

            **Cycle Length:** Triennial (3-year cycle)

            **Reregistration Timing:** At regular intervals set by the Board, no less than one time per annum

            **Statutory Language (59 OK Stat § 495a.1(A)):**
            > "At regular intervals set by the State Board of Medical Licensure and Supervision, no less than one time per annum, each licensee licensed by the Oklahoma Allopathic Medical and Surgical Licensure and Supervision Act shall demonstrate to the Board the licensee's continuing qualification to practice medicine and surgery."

            > ### Mandatory CME Topic: Pain Management & Opioid Education
            >
            > **Requirement:** Minimum 1 hour of education in pain management OR 1 hour of education in opioid use/addiction per year
            >
            > **Frequency:** Annual (prior to each renewal application)

              ## Section G: Special Requirements & Exemptions

              ### Pain Management/Opioid Requirement Exemption

              **Exemption Criteria:**
              - Licensee must demonstrate to Board's satisfaction that they do NOT hold a valid federal DEA registration number
              - - Exception applies to both pain management and opioid education hours
               
                - **Source:** 59-495a.1(C)
               
                - ### Substance Abuse Rehabilitation Exception
               
                - **Reporting Exception:**
                - - Licensees in Board-approved rehabilitation program for alcohol/substance addiction are exempt from disclosure of substance abuse during previous registration period
                 
                  - **Source:** 59-495a.1(A)(7)
                 
                  - ### Evidence Classification

                      ## Section H: Renewal Process Workflow

                      ### Phase 1: Initial Notification
                      1. Board mails renewal notice to licensee
                      2. 2. Notice includes renewal deadline and required forms
                         3. 3. Licensee receives OBMLS portal access instructions
                           
                            4. ### Phase 2: Application Preparation
                            5. 1. Licensee logs into OBMLS renewal portal
                               2. 2. Completes reregistration form(s)
                                  3. 3. Updates professional information and practice details
                                     4. 4. Reports all required disclosures
                                        5. 5. Provides documentation of CME completion (60 hours for 3-year cycle)
                                           6. 6. Verifies pain management/opioid education requirement (1 hour per year unless DEA exemption)
                                             
                                              7. ### Phase 3: Submission & Attestation
                                              8. 1. Licensee reviews all information for accuracy
                                                 2. 2. Licensee signs and attests to veracity of application
                                                    3. 3. Application submitted through OBMLS portal
                                                       4. 4. Licensee receives confirmation of submission
                                                         
                                                          5. ### Phase 4: Board Review
                                                          6. 1. Board reviews reregistration forms
                                                             2. 2. Board may request additional documentation
                                                                3. 3. Board may initiate investigations based on disclosed information
                                                                   4. 4. Board evaluates licensee fitness to continue practice
                                                                     
                                                                      5. ### Phase 5: License Issuance
                                                                      6. 1. Upon Board finding that licensee is "fit to continue to practice medicine and surgery"
                                                                         2. 2. Board issues renewal license for next registration period
                                                                            3. 3. License information updated on Board website (within 24 hours)
                                                                              
                                                                               4. ### Phase 6: Post-Renewal
                                                                               5. 1. Updated license information available on public search via okmedicalboard.org
                                                                                  2. 2. License valid for duration of registration period set by Board
